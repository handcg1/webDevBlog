This week I decided to look into image mapping. Image mapping allows a user to click on different parts of an image to see more information or be redirected. To learn about image mapping, I followed a tutorial from w3schools which can be found here. I found image mapping to be a fun way to interact with pictures beyond just looking at them. I think this feature can be really useful in a lot of different ways. For example, it could be used for a museum website where users can click on different parts of an image to get more information about the image or zoom in. Image mapping could also be used for a website that sells clothes. Users could click on an article of clothing they liked and then be redirected to a page where they could purchase that piece of clothing. In my implementation I have a picture of Isabell, the dog I am fostering. You can click on her left ear, and nose to learn more about her.

Image mapping works by creating a base image and then linking it to the map by using the usemap attribute as seen below. 

 
            <img id="mapped" src="https://live.staticflickr.com/65535/50346331603_93ab984b1a_b.jpg" alt="German shepherd sittng on bed" usemap="#map">

The map is created by using the map tag with different areas inside of it as seen below. 

 
            <map name="map">
            <area id="left-ear" shape="circle" coords="128,130,40" alt="Dog head"  onclick="show('ear')">
            </map>

These areas can have different shapes such as circle or square and the coords attribute will change based on the specific shape. One difficulty I had with image mapping was that the base image could not be resized to fit the browser because then the image map would be off. Another challenge I faced was that it was hard to figure out the coordinates to put the areas in the right positions. I ended up having to do a lot of guessing and checking. Additionally, I also wish it was easier to create a hover effect on the areas so the user could tell where the area is and where they can click. I looked into this issue and found a stack overflow article that said the html area element does not accept hovering. I tried to implement their solution which involved Jquery but I ran into trouble getting it to work.
